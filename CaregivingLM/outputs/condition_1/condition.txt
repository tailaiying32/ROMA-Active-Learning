The patient is a 62-year-old male, 9 months post left MCA ischemic stroke, presenting with mild right hemiparesis. Active range of motion is functionally full in all joints with the exception of mild weakness in right shoulder abduction and fine motor coordination deficits in the right hand. No spasticity is observed (MAS = 0). Strength is graded 4/5 in right upper limb globally, with MMT 3+ for intrinsic hand muscles. Sensory function is intact. The patient demonstrates slow but independent performance of ADLs using the right hand, but fatigues with extended bimanual tasks such as dressing. He compensates with prolonged use of the left hand. Seated balance is excellent.
