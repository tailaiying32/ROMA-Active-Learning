The patient is a 70-year-old male, 3 years post pontine stroke, with tetraparesis more pronounced in the upper limbs. Voluntary movement in both arms is severely restricted; shoulder flexion is trace (1/5 bilaterally), and finger function is absent. No functional grasp or reach present. Spasticity is intermittent, triggered by high-velocity stretch or cold contact. The patient uses a power wheelchair and communicates verbally but has delayed reaction time and mild apraxia. Head and eye control are preserved. He depends on full assistance for all upper-limb ADLs. Adaptive equipment (universal cuff, mobile arm support) was trialed without success due to limited initiation and endurance.
