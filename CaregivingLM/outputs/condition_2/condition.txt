This is a 58-year-old female, 5 months post right MCA infarct with resultant moderate left hemiparesis, complicated by spasticity in the left upper extremity (Modified Ashworth Scale 2 at elbow and wrist flexors) and partial shoulder subluxation. Voluntary shoulder flexion is limited to ~80°, with minimal active hand movement. Elbow flexion is preserved to ~100° with significant tone. Finger function is non-functional. There is neglect of the left visual field and decreased proprioceptive feedback in the affected arm. Sitting balance is fair, with postural asymmetry noted. She requires moderate assistance with dressing and grooming. Adaptive techniques (e.g., dressing affected side first, one-handed strategies) are inconsistently successful due to cognitive-perceptual limitations.
