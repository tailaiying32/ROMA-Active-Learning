# Dressing Task Analysis

## Functional Abilities Required
- Shoulder ROM (flexion, abduction, ER) for upper-body dressing
- Hip/knee ROM and reach for lower-body dressing
- Bimanual coordination (managing fabric, donning sleeves/pants)
- Hand strength and precision (buttoning, zipping)
- Trunk control and sequencing

## User Limitations That Matter Most
- Limited reach (ROM or weakness)
- Hemiparesis or one-hand use
- Fine motor limitations (buttons, zippers)
- Poor sitting/standing balance

## Variation in Assistance Strategies
- **Mild impairment**: Dressing aids (button hooks, reachers)
- **Moderate impairment**: Adaptive clothing (Velcro closures), dressing in seated position
- **Severe impairment**: Stepwise physical assistance, side-lying dressing techniques

## Robot Adaptation Considerations
- Movement speed should be slow and controlled when assisting with affected limbs
- Trajectory planning should avoid overhead reach for users with limited shoulder flexion
- Grasp assistance should focus on stabilizing clothing rather than pulling limbs
- Postural support may be needed for users with poor trunk control
- Communication should guide users through the dressing sequence step-by-step
