# Hygiene Task Analysis

## Functional Abilities Required
- Shoulder and elbow ROM for reaching face, hair, and body areas
- Hand grasp and manipulation for holding hygiene tools
- Trunk control and balance for seated or standing hygiene
- Coordination for bilateral tasks (washing, drying)
- Fine motor skills for detailed hygiene tasks

## User Limitations That Matter Most
- Limited reach due to ROM restrictions or weakness
- Poor hand strength or coordination for tool manipulation
- Balance issues affecting seated or standing hygiene
- Cognitive deficits affecting task sequencing
- Sensory impairments affecting hygiene awareness

## Variation in Assistance Strategies
- **Mild impairment**: Adaptive tools (long-handled brushes, electric toothbrushes)
- **Moderate impairment**: Seated hygiene with grab bars, adapted tools
- **Severe impairment**: Assisted hygiene with caregiver support, specialized equipment

## Robot Adaptation Considerations
- Movement speed should be gentle and controlled for hygiene tasks
- Trajectory planning should respect personal space and comfort zones
- Grasp assistance should support tool holding without causing discomfort
- Postural support may be needed for users with balance issues
- Communication should be respectful and maintain dignity during personal care tasks
