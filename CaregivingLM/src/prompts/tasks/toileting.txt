# Toileting Task Analysis

## Functional Abilities Required
- Lower extremity strength for transfers and positioning
- Trunk control and balance for seated toileting
- Hand function for clothing management and hygiene
- Mobility for bathroom access and positioning
- Cognitive awareness for toileting needs and safety

## User Limitations That Matter Most
- Poor lower extremity strength affecting transfers
- Balance issues affecting seated stability
- Limited hand function for clothing management
- Mobility restrictions affecting bathroom access
- Cognitive deficits affecting toileting awareness

## Variation in Assistance Strategies
- **Mild impairment**: Grab bars, raised toilet seats, adaptive clothing
- **Moderate impairment**: Transfer assistance, commode chairs, caregiver setup
- **Severe impairment**: Full transfer assistance, specialized toileting equipment

## Robot Adaptation Considerations
- Movement speed should be very slow and controlled for transfers
- Trajectory planning should prioritize safety and stability
- Grasp assistance should provide secure support during transfers
- Postural support is critical for maintaining balance during toileting
- Communication should be clear and maintain dignity during personal care
