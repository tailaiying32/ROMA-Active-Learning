# Transferring Task Analysis

## Functional Abilities Required
- Lower extremity strength for standing and weight bearing
- Trunk control and balance for transfer movements
- Upper extremity strength for support and propulsion
- Coordination for smooth transfer sequences
- Cognitive awareness for safety during transfers

## User Limitations That Matter Most
- Poor lower extremity strength affecting standing ability
- Balance issues affecting transfer stability
- Limited upper extremity strength for support
- Coordination deficits affecting transfer smoothness
- Cognitive deficits affecting transfer safety

## Variation in Assistance Strategies
- **Mild impairment**: Stand-by assistance, grab bars, transfer boards
- **Moderate impairment**: Physical assistance, mechanical lifts, slide boards
- **Severe impairment**: Full mechanical lift assistance, specialized transfer equipment

## Robot Adaptation Considerations
- Movement speed should be extremely slow and controlled for transfers
- Trajectory planning should prioritize stability and safety
- Grasp assistance should provide secure support throughout the transfer
- Postural support is critical for maintaining balance during transfers
- Communication should be clear and provide step-by-step guidance
