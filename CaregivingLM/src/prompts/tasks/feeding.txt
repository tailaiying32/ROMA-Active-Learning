# Feeding Task Analysis

## Functional Abilities Required
- Shoulder and elbow ROM (flexion, abduction, supination)
- Hand grasp and release (palmar or lateral pinch)
- Oral-motor coordination (if self-spooning to mouth)
- Trunk stability (seated posture)
- Coordination for utensil handling

## User Limitations That Matter Most
- Limited active elbow flexion or supination
- Poor grip strength or finger extension
- Involuntary movements (e.g., tremors, spasticity)
- Inattention or cognitive sequencing deficits

## Variation in Assistance Strategies
- **Mild impairment**: Adaptive utensils (angled spoon, built-up handle), plate guards
- **Moderate impairment**: Mobile arm support, universal cuff, bowl stabilization
- **Severe impairment**: Hand-over-hand assistance, robot-assisted spoon transport, feeding from a pre-positioned spoon

## Robot Adaptation Considerations
- Movement speed should be very slow and precise for utensil manipulation
- Trajectory planning should account for tremors and involuntary movements
- Grasp assistance should provide gentle support without taking over the task
- Postural support is critical for maintaining upright seated position
- Communication should be encouraging and provide clear instructions for each step
